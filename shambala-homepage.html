Where the Lake
Holds Still

Shambala Holistic & Retreat Centre sits alongside the shores of Upper Lough Erne — a sanctuary carved from the landscape of Co. Fermanagh, where the water is calm and the pace of the world slows to match it.

Founded by Sukhada Smith-Repass in 1999, Shambala has offered holistic therapies, residential retreats, sound healing and yoga to guests from across Ireland and beyond for over 26 years. Every session, every retreat, is rooted in genuine practice — not wellness as a trend, but as a way of life.

We treat people as individuals, not symptoms. We welcome guests from Northern Ireland, the Republic of Ireland, and anyone who needs to find their way back to stillness.

The Setting

Upper Lough Erne,
Co. Fermanagh

Shambala sits on the banks of Upper Lough Erne — one of Ireland's most beautiful and least-visited landscapes. The lake is a constant presence: visible from the yoga space, audible from the treatment rooms, steps from the front door.

We are 20 minutes from Enniskillen, 40 minutes from Cavan, and accessible from Dublin and Belfast within two hours. The border is crossed without formality — guests from the Republic are welcome, and we price in both currencies as a matter of course.

"Easily reached from Cavan, Monaghan, Leitrim, Derry and Belfast — a true all-Ireland retreat, sitting quietly at the heart of the island."

Sound Healing · Northern Ireland

What Is a
Sound Bath?

A sound bath is a meditative experience in which you are immersed in the resonant tones of Tibetan singing bowls, crystal bowls, and gong. You lie down, close your eyes, and let the sound do its work.

The vibrations slow brainwave activity into a deeply relaxed, meditative state — reducing stress, easing anxiety, and restoring the body's natural equilibrium. No experience necessary. No effort required.
