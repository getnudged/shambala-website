A wellness retreat gives you something that is increasingly rare: protected time away from ordinary demands. Instead of fitting rest around everything else, you make rest the centre of the day.

At Shambala Holistic & Retreat Centre in Derrylin, retreats are shaped by the lakeside setting, holistic therapies, yoga, meditation, sound healing, nourishing food and the quiet rhythm of Upper Lough Erne.

## 1. You Step Out of Daily Routine

Most people are carrying more than they realise. Work, family, screens, travel and constant decision-making can keep the nervous system switched on.

A retreat creates a clear boundary. You arrive, unpack, breathe, and let the pace change. That simple shift can be powerful before any formal session begins.

## 2. The Setting Supports the Work

Upper Lough Erne is part of the experience at Shambala. The water, trees and open sky give the body constant cues to slow down.

Wellness is easier to access when the place itself is calm. A retreat in Fermanagh offers space without needing to travel far from Northern Ireland or the border counties.

## 3. You Can Try More Than One Practice

A retreat can combine yoga, meditation, sound bath, forest bathing, mindful movement and holistic therapies. This gives you several ways into rest rather than relying on one approach.

Some guests connect most with movement. Others soften through sound, nature, Reiki or simply having quiet time between sessions.

## 4. You Are Looked After

On retreat, meals, structure and timing are held for you. This matters. When you do not have to plan every part of the day, attention can return to the body and breath.

Shambala retreats are intentionally small and personal, with a holistic approach that treats each guest as an individual.

## 5. You Return With Something Practical

A good retreat is not an escape from life. It gives you practices and reminders you can bring home: slower breathing, better rest, clearer boundaries, more awareness of what restores you.

Even a short weekend can help you notice what your body has been asking for.
